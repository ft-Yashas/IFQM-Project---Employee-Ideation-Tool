## 1. What is IFQM?

IFQM is a platform that helps companies collect and manage ideas from their employees. Think of it as a suggestion box that is digital, smarter, and tracks every idea from start to finish.

**What you can do on IFQM:**
- Submit ideas for improvements at work
- Track the status of your ideas
- Earn points and rewards for good ideas
- See what others are submitting
- Vote on ideas you like

---

## 2. User Roles — What Can I Do?

| Your Role | What You Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Employee / Trainee** | Submit ideas, track your own ideas, vote on other ideas, comment on ideas |
| **Team Lead / Project Lead** | Everything above + review ideas from your team members, approve or reject ideas |
| **Manager / Senior Manager** | Everything above + review ideas escalated to you, see your team's performance |
| **Executive** | All reviewer abilities + see the full organization picture, final approval authority |
| **Organization Admin** | Everything + manage users (add/remove), change organization settings, see all ideas |
| **Organization Super Admin** | Same as Org Admin but with full control over the organization's account |
| **Platform Admin** | (IFQM team only) Can create new organizations, view aggregate stats across all orgs — cannot see individual idea content |

> **Note:** Your organization admin decides who has which role. Contact them if you have questions about your access level.

## 3. How to Log In

### For Organization Users (employees, managers, admins):

1. Open the IFQM URL provided by your organization
2. You will see a **login screen** with fields for:
   - **Email Address** — your work email (e.g., john.smith@company.com)
   - **Password** — your login password
   - **Organization Code** — a short code for your company (e.g., `acme` or `jain`). Ask your admin if you don't know this. You can leave it blank only if your admin says so.
3. Click **Sign In**

### For Platform Admins (IFQM team only):

1. Open the IFQM login page
2. Enter your platform admin email and password
3. **Leave the Organization Code blank** — this tells the system you are a platform-level admin
4. Click **Sign In**

> **Tip:** If you forget your password, contact your organization admin (or the IFQM platform admin for platform-level accounts).

## 5. Submitting an Idea

### Step-by-step process:

**Step 1 — Title**
Give your idea a short, clear name. This is what people will see first. Example: *"Reduce wait time at the cafeteria"*

**Step 2 — Present Situation (What is the problem?)**
Describe the current problem in detail. Who does it affect? How often does it happen? What is the impact? Example: *"Employees spend 20 minutes waiting in line at the cafeteria during lunch hours. This reduces productivity as people come back late to their desks."*

**Step 3 — Proposed Solution**
Describe your idea for solving the problem. Be specific about how it would work. Example: *"Introduce a pre-order mobile app where employees can order their lunch in advance. This reduces wait time to under 5 minutes."*

**Step 4 — Impact Areas**
Select which areas of the business your idea would improve. You can select multiple:
- Quality (improving product/service quality)
- Cost (reducing expenses)
- Time (saving time for employees)
- Safety (making the workplace safer)
- Revenue (increasing sales or income)
- Customer Experience (improving how customers feel)

Also rate the **Impact Level** — Low, Medium, or High — based on how big of an improvement your idea would make.

**Step 5 — Review & Submit**
- Review everything you've written
- Optionally attach files (images, documents) to support your idea
- Click **Submit** to send your idea for review

> **AI Quality Scoring:** When you submit, the system automatically analyzes your idea and gives it an AI score (0-100). A higher score means your idea is well-written and clearly explained. This score helps reviewers prioritize.

**What happens after you submit:**
1. You will see a confirmation message
2. You earn **10 points** for submitting
3. Your idea goes to the first reviewer in your organization's approval chain
4. You will receive a **notification** when there is an update on your idea (approved, rejected, or needs more information)

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | You saved the idea but haven't submitted it yet. You can continue editing it. |
| **Submitted** | Your idea has been submitted and is waiting for the first reviewer to act on it. |
| **Under Review** | Your idea is being reviewed by one or more people in the approval chain. |
| **Approved** | Your idea has been accepted! It may be implemented in the future. |

Click on any idea to see its full details, review history, comments, and any points you've earned.

## 7. The Review Process — What Happens After You Submit?

### The Approval Chain (How Ideas Move Up)

When you submit an idea, it doesn't go to just one person. It follows a chain of reviewers set by your organization admin.

**Default Approval Chain (Platform Default):**

```
You submit an idea
       ↓
  Team Lead reviews
       ↓ (if approved)
  Project Lead reviews
       ↓ (if approved)
    Manager reviews
       ↓ (if approved)
Senior Manager reviews
       ↓ (if approved)
  Executive / Admin gives FINAL decision (Approved or Rejected)
```

**What each reviewer does:**
- **Approve** — Move the idea to the next level in the chain
- **Reject** — The idea is rejected. You will be notified with the reason.
- **Comment** — Ask you a question or give feedback without making a decision yet

**Escalation:**
If a reviewer at any level approves an idea and there is a higher role above them in the chain, the idea automatically moves up to the next reviewer. You will be notified each time your idea escalates.

### Multi-Reviewer Mode (Committee Review)

Instead of a chain, some ideas may be sent to a **group of reviewers at once** (like a committee). Each reviewer votes:
- **Approve** — I agree with this idea
- **Reject** — I don't agree with this idea

The idea is approved only when enough people vote yes (based on a threshold set by your org admin).

> **Who decides which mode is used?** The organization admin decides the default. However, the reviewer can also choose to convert an individual idea to committee review if needed.

## 8. For Managers & Reviewers — Your Review Queue

**When you have ideas to review**, you will see a badge on the **Review Queue** item in the sidebar showing how many ideas need your attention.

### In the Review Queue:

1. **You see all ideas** that are assigned to you (either because you are the current reviewer in the chain, or because you are a committee member for that idea)
2. Click on any idea to open the full details and review panel
3. Choose your action: **Approve**, **Reject**, or **Add Comment**

### Review Actions:

**Approve:**
- In a chain workflow: the idea moves to the next reviewer in the chain
- In committee mode: your "approve" vote is recorded
- You can optionally add a comment explaining your decision

**Reject:**
- The idea is rejected and the submitter is notified
- You must provide a reason (comment) when rejecting
- The idea does not move further up the chain

**Escalate (automatic):**
- If you approve and there is a higher-level reviewer in the chain, the idea automatically escalates to them
- You don't need to do anything extra — the system handles it

**Re-open:**
- If an idea was previously rejected, a reviewer with sufficient authority can re-open it for reconsideration

## 9. Committee Review (Multi-Reviewer Mode)

In this mode, an idea is sent to **multiple reviewers at the same time** — like a committee making a group decision.

### How it works:

1. The idea is assigned to 3, 5, or however many reviewers the org admin or a senior reviewer decides
2. Each reviewer independently votes **Approve** or **Reject**
3. The final decision depends on the **approval threshold** set for the idea

**Example:** If the threshold is 60% and there are 5 reviewers, the idea needs at least 3 approvals (60% of 5 = 3) to be approved. If even 1 reviewer rejects and the threshold is 100%, the idea is rejected.

**Who can convert an idea to committee review?**
- Organization admins
- Any reviewer in the chain who decides the idea needs broader input

## 10. Customizing Your Organization's Approval Flow

*(This section is for Organization Admins only)*

Your organization may not follow the default 4-step approval chain. IFQM lets you customize the approval flow to match your actual organizational structure.

### How to Change the Approval Flow:

1. Go to **Admin Panel** in the sidebar
2. Click the **Org Settings** tab
3. Scroll down to the **Approval Workflow** section

### What You Can Customize:

#### Option 1: Use Platform Default
- Select **"Use Platform Default"** — this keeps the standard 4-step chain (Team Lead → Project Lead → Manager → Senior Manager → Executive/Admin final)
- This is the simplest option and works for most organizations

#### Option 2: Customize My Approval Hierarchy
- Select **"Customize My Approval Hierarchy"** to reveal more options

**Reviewer Roles (Escalation Chain):**
- Check the boxes for each role that should be in your approval chain
- Ideas will move through only the selected roles in order
- Example: If you only check "Manager", ideas go directly to the manager — team leads are skipped
- Example: If you check "Team Lead" and "Executive", ideas go to the team lead first, then directly to an executive for final decision

**Final Approver Roles:**
- These roles can give a **final** decision (Approve or Reject) — the idea does NOT escalate further after reaching them
- By default, Executive, Admin, and Super Admin are final approvers

**Default Approval Threshold (for committee mode):**
- A percentage (1-100%) — this controls what % of committee members must approve for the idea to pass
- Default is 100% (everyone must approve)

### Resetting to Platform Defaults:
Click the **"Reset to Platform Defaults"** button at any time to restore the standard 4-step chain.

> **Tip:** Before changing the approval flow, make sure your organization has users in the roles you select. If you select a role that nobody has, ideas may get stuck with no reviewer available.

## 11. Points & Rewards

IFQM gamifies idea submission by awarding **points** to employees who contribute good ideas.

### How Points Work:

| Action | Points Earned |
|---|---|
| Submit an idea | +10 points |
| Idea gets Approved | +25 points |
| Idea gets Implemented (put into practice) | +65 points |

**Points accumulate** over time. You can see your total points on your profile and in the Leaderboard.

**Why points matter:**
- Points show your contribution to organizational improvement
- Top contributors are celebrated on the Leaderboard
- Some organizations may tie points to real rewards or recognition programs — ask your admin

## 12. Leaderboard

The **Leaderboard** shows the top idea contributors in your organization.

**How it works:**
- Employees are ranked by their total points
- It shows how many ideas each person submitted, how many were approved, and how many were implemented
- It also shows the **average quality score** (AI score) of each person's ideas

**Filters:**
- You can filter the leaderboard by **department** or **business unit** to see team-specific rankings

## 13. Challenges — Time-Bound Idea Campaigns

**Challenges** are time-limited idea campaigns set by your organization admin. They focus employee attention on specific problems or themes.

### How Challenges Work:

1. Your organization admin creates a **Challenge** with:
   - A title (e.g., *"How can we reduce waste in our production process?"*)
   - A description with more details
   - A **deadline** (last date to submit ideas)
2. When a challenge is active, you will see a banner on your dashboard
3. You can submit ideas specifically linked to the challenge
4. Ideas submitted before the deadline and linked to the challenge are automatically included in the challenge evaluation

**Why challenges matter:**
- Ideas submitted for a challenge may get **priority review**
- Some organizations award bonus points for challenge-winning ideas
- Challenges make it easy to focus everyone's creativity on one problem

> **Challenges are optional** — you can still submit regular ideas outside of challenges at any time (if your admin has enabled the feature).

## 14. For Organization Admins — Managing Your Team

*(This section is for Organization Admins and Super Admins)*

### User Management

In the **Admin Panel → User Management** tab, you can:
- **Add new users** — Enter their name, email, employee ID, department, and assign a role
- **Edit existing users** — Change their department, role, or reporting manager
- **Deactivate users** — Remove access for employees who leave (their ideas remain in the system)

### Points Configuration

In **Admin Panel → Points Config** tab, you can see how many points are awarded for each action. (The actual point values are set at the platform level — contact the IFQM team if you need to change them.)

### Organization Settings

In **Admin Panel → Org Settings** tab, you can configure:
- Review SLA (how many days a reviewer has to act on an idea)
- Escalation threshold (how many days before an idea automatically escalates)
- Feature toggles (enable/disable anonymous submissions, public board, challenges, email notifications)
- Email/SMTP settings
- **Approval workflow** (customize the reviewer chain as described in Section 10)

### Database Status

In **Admin Panel → DB Status** tab, you can see information about your organization's database and HR sync status.

### Rescoring Ideas

In **Admin Panel → Rescore Ideas** tab, you can re-run AI quality scoring on all existing ideas. Use this when you want to refresh scores after the AI model has been improved.

## 15. For Platform Admins — Managing All Organizations

*(This section is for the IFQM platform team only — not for organization admins)*

Platform Admins have access to a separate dashboard (shown when you log in **without** an organization code).

### Platform Dashboard

**Overview Tab:**
- Total number of organizations on the platform
- Total users across all organizations
- Total ideas submitted across the platform
- Activity timeline showing recent events

**Tenants Tab:**
- See a list of all organizations (tenants) on the platform
- Each organization shows: user count, idea count, implementation rate, last activity date
- **Create New Organization** — Use this to set up a new organization on the platform
- **View Organization** — See the structure and stats of any specific organization

### Creating a New Organization

1. Click **New Organisation** on the Tenants tab
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Organization Name** — The name of the company (e.g., "Acme Manufacturing")
   - **Slug** — A short code that becomes part of their login URL (auto-generated from name)
   - **Admin Name** — The name of the first admin for this organization
   - **Admin Email** — The email for the first admin
   - **Admin Password** — The password for the first admin
   - **Primary Color** — A color theme for the organization's dashboard (optional)
3. Click **Create** — The system will:
   - Create a new database for the organization
   - Set up all the required tables
   - Create the first admin user
   - Register the organization on the platform

### What Platform Admins CANNOT Do:

**Privacy Contract:** Platform Admins can only see **aggregate statistics** (total users, total ideas, implementation rates). They **cannot** see the content of individual ideas, individual user performance, or any confidential information within an organization. Each organization's data is private and isolated.

## 16. Common Questions & Troubleshooting

### I submitted an idea but haven't heard back. What do I do?
Check the status in **My Ideas**. If it's been more than the configured SLA days (usually 7 days), contact your direct manager or the first reviewer in the chain.

### Can I edit an idea after submitting it?
You can **add comments** to clarify, but you cannot edit the idea content once submitted. If you need major changes, contact your admin to reject the idea so you can re-submit.

### Can I submit an idea anonymously?
Yes, if your organization admin has enabled **anonymous submissions** in Org Settings. In that case, a checkbox will appear on the submission form.

### Can I submit an idea on behalf of someone else?
No. Each idea is submitted by one person, but you can list **co-suggesters** (up to 2 other people) who contributed to the idea. Both the submitter and co-suggesters earn points.

### I got my idea rejected. Can I resubmit?
Yes. You can re-submit a revised version of the idea. The system will treat it as a completely new submission.

### How do I change my password?
Contact your organization admin to reset your password.

### I'm an org admin but I don't see the Approval Workflow section.
Make sure you are logged in with an **admin** or **super_admin** role. Go to **Admin Panel → Org Settings** tab.

### The leaderboard shows someone with more points than me, but I submitted more ideas. Why?
Points are awarded for outcomes, not just submission. The person with fewer ideas but more implemented ideas will have more points.

## 17. Glossary — Words Explained

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Approval Chain** | The sequence of reviewers an idea goes through before getting a final decision |
| **Approval Threshold** | In committee review: the percentage of reviewers who must approve for the idea to pass |
| **Escalation** | When an idea moves from one reviewer to the next higher-level reviewer in the chain |
| **Hierarchical Workflow** | A chain-based review where ideas move one step at a time through the org chart |
| **Multi-Reviewer Workflow** | Committee-based review where multiple reviewers vote simultaneously |
| **Org Settings** | Configuration options that the organization admin can customize |
| **Reviewer** | A person in the approval chain who can approve, reject, or comment on ideas |
| **SLA (Service Level Agreement)** | The number of days a reviewer has to act on an idea before it's considered overdue |
| **Super Admin** | The highest-level user within a specific organization (not the platform) |
| **Platform Admin** | The IFQM team member who manages all organizations on the platform |
| **Tenant** | Another word for "organization" on the IFQM platform |
| **AI Score** | A quality score (0-100) given by the AI to each submitted idea based on how clearly and thoroughly it is written |
| **Co-Suggester** | A second or third person who contributed to an idea (listed during submission) |
| **Challenge** | A time-bound campaign focused on a specific theme or problem |
| **ROI** | Return on Investment — a measure of how much financial or practical benefit the idea will deliver |
